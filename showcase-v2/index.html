Part 1 · The Golfer

01

Pick your course

  • GPS finds courses near you — Siena GC shows as Live, ready to order.
  • Search across 14,000+ US courses for everyone else — the waitlist funnel.
  • One tap and the app is themed to your course for the rest of the round.
02

Home — the live course map

  • One dominant action: "Order to my hole." Nothing competes with it.
  • Live runner positions with ETAs — golfers see the 4-minute promise before ordering.
  • Status chip reads like a heartbeat: runners on course, free now, ETA.
  • Quick reorder puts your usual one tap away.
03

Menu — browse like Apple Music

  • Duotone category tiles — Beer, Cocktails, Snacks, Non-Alcoholic — tap to filter.
  • Compact rows fit 8 items per screen; price and add button always visible.
  • "Most ordered" leads with what actually sells at this course.
  • Floating cart pill follows you — total always one glance away.
04

Build your drink

  • Spirit → brand → mixer in three taps; price updates live with each choice.
  • "Your build" recipe card shows exactly what the runner pours.
  • Upsell is built in: premium brands carry their own price bumps.
05

Checkout

  • Flat $2 delivery to your tee box — visible, honest pricing.
  • GPS auto-delivery: the drop point moves with the golfer, no pin-dropping.
  • 21+ ID verification promise right on the pay button — compliance as a feature.
06

Confirmed & live tracking

  • Uber-style: countdown ETA, progress bar, runner card with rating.
  • Four-step status keeps golfers informed without them asking the cart girl "where's my drink."
  • Message the runner directly if plans change mid-hole.
07

Activity — history & one-tap reorder

  • Season stats up top: orders, average delivery, total spent.
  • Every past order is a reorder button — the repeat-purchase engine.
  • Today's live order sits first with its status.
08

Account

  • ID verified once, badge forever — no re-carding every order.
  • Apple Pay default, home course, notifications, receipts.

Part 2 · The Staff

09

Runner — queue

  • Uber-driver model: incoming request with a 24-second accept window.
  • Earnings, deliveries, and average time front and center — gamified hustle.
  • Active route stacks the next stops in order; restock alerts before they run dry.
10

Runner — route

  • Cart-path routing to the golfer's live GPS position, not a static hole number.
  • The order manifest rides along — what to pour before arriving.
  • One button state machine: Arrived → verify ID → complete.
11

Runner — 21+ ID check

  • Three-item checklist, optional barcode scan auto-fills it.
  • Failed check protocol baked in: alcohol returns, food still delivers, drink auto-refunds. No arguments at the tee box.
  • This screen is the liability armor the course's lawyers will ask about.
12

Manager — live dashboard

  • Revenue, order count, and delivery speed for the day at a glance.
  • Orders-by-hour chart shows the rush; runner board shows who's where.
  • Top seller with an "86 it" button — kill an item the moment it runs out.
